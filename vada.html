Vada Recipe

Description

Vada is a popular South Indian snack that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Made from urad dal (black gram lentils) and spices, it is typically served with chutneys and sambar. It is a favorite for breakfast or as a tea-time snack.

About Vada

Vada is a traditional South Indian dish known for its unique texture and flavor. It is made by grinding soaked urad dal into a thick batter, which is then spiced and shaped into round fritters. The fritters are deep-fried until golden brown. Vada has a rich history and is often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions. It is usually paired with coconut chutney and sambar, making it a delicious and satisfying meal.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup curry leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Soak the urad dal in water for about 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the dal and grind it into a smooth batter. The batter should be thick and slightly coarse.
  3. Add the chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, coriander leaves, and salt to the batter. Mix well.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Take a small amount of batter and shape it into a round ball, then flatten it slightly. You can use your fingers or a spoon.
  6. Carefully slide the vada into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
