Murukku Recipe

About Murukku

Murukku is a popular South Indian snack that is crispy and savory. It is made from rice flour and urad dal flour, seasoned with spices, and then deep-fried. It is often enjoyed with tea or as an accompaniment to meals.

Description

Murukku, also known as Chakli in some regions, is a traditional snack made during festivals and special occasions in South India. It has a crunchy texture and is typically spiced with cumin seeds, sesame seeds, and ajwain. The dough is shaped into spirals or loops and deep-fried until golden brown. Murukku can be stored for several weeks in an airtight container, making it a convenient and delicious snack to have on hand.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup urad dal flour
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine rice flour, urad dal flour, cumin seeds, sesame seeds, ajwain, chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt.
  2. Gradually add water and mix to form a smooth dough. The dough should be pliable but not too soft.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  4. Using a murukku maker or a piping bag with a star nozzle, pipe the dough into spiral shapes or loops directly into the hot oil.
  5. Fry until the murukku turns golden brown and crispy, then remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  6. Allow the murukku to cool completely before storing in an airtight container.
