Meen Varuval Recipe

Description

Meen Varuval is a popular South Indian dish made with marinated fish pieces that are fried to perfection. The dish is known for its spicy and tangy flavor, achieved through a blend of aromatic spices and herbs. It's often enjoyed as a side dish with rice and sambar or as a standalone snack.

About Meen Varuval

Meen Varuval is a traditional Tamil Nadu recipe that showcases the rich culinary heritage of South India. The dish is typically made with a variety of fish, including seer fish or tilapia, which is marinated in a mixture of spices such as turmeric, chili powder, and coriander, and then shallow-fried until crispy. It is a favorite among seafood lovers and is often served during festive occasions and family gatherings.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g fish fillets (seer fish or tilapia)
  • 2 tablespoons red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Clean and pat dry the fish fillets.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, fennel seeds, cumin seeds, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, and salt to make a marinade.
  3. Coat the fish fillets with the marinade and let them sit for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  5. Place the marinated fish fillets in the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  6. Remove the fish from the pan and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Serve hot with rice or as a snack.
