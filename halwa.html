Halwa Recipe

Description

Halwa is a traditional sweet dish popular in South Asian cuisine. It's known for its rich, sweet, and aromatic flavors. Made with various ingredients like semolina, lentils, or vegetables, it is often served as a dessert or during festive occasions. The dish is cooked with ghee (clarified butter), sugar, and flavored with cardamom, nuts, and raisins, making it a delicious and comforting treat.

About

Halwa has its origins in ancient Middle Eastern and South Asian cuisines. Its preparation varies by region and family tradition, but the core concept remains the same: a sweet, creamy, and flavorful dessert. The recipe has been passed down through generations, and each region has its own unique twist on the classic dish. Whether enjoyed as a festive treat or a comforting dessert, Halwa continues to be a beloved part of many culinary traditions.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (cashews, almonds)
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the semolina and roast it, stirring continuously, until it turns golden brown and fragrant.
  3. In a separate pan, bring water to a boil and then slowly pour it into the roasted semolina while stirring to avoid lumps.
  4. Add sugar and continue to cook, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan.
  5. Add cardamom powder, chopped nuts, and raisins. Stir well and cook for another few minutes until the nuts are toasted and the raisins are plump.
  6. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving. Enjoy warm or at room temperature.
