Athirasam

Description

Athirasam, also known as “Ariselu” or “Adirasam,” is a traditional South Indian sweet made from rice flour and jaggery. It is deep-fried to achieve a crispy texture on the outside while remaining soft and chewy on the inside. This sweet treat is often prepared during festivals and special occasions, beloved for its rich flavor and delightful crunch.

About Athirasam

Athirasam is a cherished delicacy in South Indian cuisine, particularly popular in Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh. The name "Athirasam" translates to “sweet rice cake” in Tamil. It is a part of traditional festive sweets and is often made during celebrations such as Diwali and Pongal. The preparation involves soaking rice, grinding it to a fine paste, and then mixing it with jaggery syrup to form a dough. This dough is then shaped into discs and deep-fried to perfection.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 cup jaggery
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp sesame seeds (optional)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice in water for 2 hours. Drain and grind the rice to a fine paste with a little water.
  2. In a pan, heat the water and add jaggery. Stir until the jaggery dissolves completely and forms a thick syrup.
  3. Add the rice paste to the jaggery syrup and cook on low heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan.
  4. Mix in cardamom powder and sesame seeds if using. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool slightly.
  5. Once cool enough to handle, shape the mixture into small discs or rings using your hands or a mold.
  6. Heat oil in a pan for deep frying. Fry the discs in hot oil until golden brown and crisp on both sides.
  7. Remove the Athirasam from the oil and drain on paper towels. Allow to cool completely before serving.
