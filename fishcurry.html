Meen Kulambu Recipe

Description

Meen Kulambu is a traditional South Indian fish curry known for its rich and spicy flavors. The curry is made with fresh fish, tamarind, and a blend of aromatic spices. It's a popular dish in Tamil Nadu and is often enjoyed with rice or dosa.

About Meen Kulambu

Meen Kulambu is a staple in South Indian cuisine, particularly in Tamil Nadu. It is cherished for its complex flavors, which come from a mixture of spices, tamarind, and curry leaves. The dish is usually prepared with various types of fish, including tilapia or catfish, and is known for its tangy and spicy taste. It is often served with steamed rice, idli, or dosa, making it a versatile meal for any time of the day.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams of fish (such as tilapia or catfish)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add finely chopped onions and curry leaves. Saut√© until the onions become translucent.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft.
  4. Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Cook for a minute until the spices release their aroma.
  5. Add the tamarind paste and a cup of water. Stir well and bring to a boil.
  6. Add the fish pieces to the pan. Cover and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.
  7. Season with salt to taste. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  8. Serve hot with rice or your favorite South Indian bread.
