Sambar Recipe

Description

Sambar is a popular South Indian dish made with lentils, vegetables, and a special blend of spices. It's commonly served with rice, idli, or dosa and is known for its tangy and spicy flavor. Sambar is a staple in South Indian cuisine and is cherished for its nutritious and comforting qualities.

About Sambar

Sambar originated from Tamil Nadu and has become an integral part of South Indian cuisine. The dish is traditionally made with toor dal (pigeon peas) and a mix of vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and beans. The unique taste of sambar comes from the use of sambar powder, tamarind, and a variety of spices. It is often enjoyed as a wholesome meal and is celebrated for its versatility and flavor.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 medium carrot, chopped
  • 1 medium potato, chopped
  • 1 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the toor dal and cook it in a pressure cooker with 3 cups of water until soft.
  2. In a large pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.
  5. Add chopped vegetables and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Add the cooked dal, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and tamarind paste. Mix well.
  7. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  8. Season with salt and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Serve hot with rice, idli, or dosa.
