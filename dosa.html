Dosa Recipe

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is crispy, golden brown, and can be enjoyed with a variety of side dishes like chutneys and sambar. This dish is not only delicious but also a great source of nutrients and energy.

About Dosa

Dosa is a traditional Indian pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). It is a staple in South Indian cuisine and is enjoyed by people of all ages. The fermentation process gives dosa its distinctive flavor and makes it slightly tangy. Dosa can be made in various styles such as plain, masala (with a spiced potato filling), and rava dosa (using semolina).

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup rice
    • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram)
    • 1/4 tsp fenugreek seeds
    • 1/2 tsp salt (or to taste)
    • Water (as needed)
    • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • Instructions:
    1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
    2. Drain the water and grind the mixture into a smooth batter, adding water as needed to achieve a thick consistency.
    3. Transfer the batter to a bowl, cover, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it has risen and is slightly bubbly.
    4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Grease with a little oil or ghee.
    5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the skillet and spread it into a thin, even circle.
    6. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the edges start to crisp up and the bottom is golden brown. Flip and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes.
    7. Remove from the skillet and serve hot with your choice of chutneys and sambar.
